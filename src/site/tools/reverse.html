Protect Your Circuits!

Never connect anything backwards again

More Diodes!

This design is a simple example of circuit protection. If you have ever connected something backwards on your breadboard, you know it can lead to disastrous results!

The circuit will demonstrate this by turning on a green LED and allowing a motor to spin when correctly powered. If you connect your battery backwards (or mix up negative and positive like I do), the red LED will turn on.

The circuit works due to the Diodes(the 1N5817 and the red LED) we place in the middle of our breadboard. As a reminder, a diode allows current to flow in only one direction, not backwards (over-simplifying). Also LEDs are diodes!

The best way to understand this circuit is to build it! You will immediately understand how current is flowing through the circuit. Swap the power supply positive and negative a few times and you will see the results.

Try connecting something other than a 5 volt motor to your green LED side. Something to note about this circuit: Its current limited by the diodes used. You can try different diodes and swap out the LEDs eventually, but check datasheets for your use case. Have Fun!
